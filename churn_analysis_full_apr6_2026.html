Qualitative signals from comments

Recurring themes across customer comments beyond the structured reason categories.

Linux support (recurring dealbreaker)

Multiple customers citing no Linux app as sole reason for leaving — going to iDrive or self-hosting. Predictable, avoidable loss.

Unlimited plan cancellation

At least one $32 MRR customer left specifically because "unlimited was cancelled" — plan change creating active churn.

Desktop app quality

"Clunky," "hasn't been updated in years," "slow to sync to new device" — repeated across solo customers. Dropbox cited as alternative.

No downgrade-to-free option

Several customers frustrated by forced cancellation — policy generating negative sentiment, no-refund complaints, and explicit statements of non-return.

Customer service response time

Live Oak Inventories (5-year customer, $20 MRR): issues unresolved "months later." One of only 2 CS-attributed churns, but avg tenure 1,982 days — most loyal cohort lost.

Proton Drive consolidation

5 customers left for Proton Drive citing VPN+storage bundling. Differentiated value prop — worth monitoring as a growing competitive threat.

What this means for your renewal flow

Win-back window

62% of churns happen within 30 days of renewal — a targeted save campaign in that window (discount, feature callout, human outreach for ≥$24 MRR) could recover material revenue

Renewal email content

The email is triggering ~44% of churns in the 15–30d window. Consider testing a value-highlight email before the invoice notice — remind customers what they've stored and synced

Early-warning segment

11 customers cancelled 181+ days before renewal — already gone in spirit. Low engagement and no recent logins are predictive signals worth acting on before they decide
